Queen’s University offers a variety of majors for undergraduate students, but this paragraph will be focused on their Computer Science and Engineering programs. The Computer Engineering program focuses on both the hardware and software aspects of computing. The engineering program offers specializations in computer architecture, computer networking, microprocessor engineering, digital systems, and software systems. There are 23 faculty and 321 students in the engineering program. An undergraduate degree in Computer Engineering from Queen's provides an opportunity to attend graduate school at Universities such as MIT and Stanford. Queen’s University offers one of the leading Computer Science programs in the world. The program was established in 1969, and is currently expanding its software design and biomedical computing facilities. The School of Computing at Queen's, in addition to instructing, also performs research in a variety of fields. These fields include Artificial Intelligence and Robotics.
